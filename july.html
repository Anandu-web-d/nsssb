ADOPTION OF GOVERNMENT L.P SCHOOL, THURUTHY

Our NSS unit successfully launched its Second School Adoption initiative by adopting the Government LP School in Thuruthy. In collaboration with SRM Engineering College, this initiative, aims to support underprivileged schools with essential resources, infrastructure, and educational materials. As part of the scheme, we introduced a scholarship of ₹1200 for all the students of the school. During our visit, NSS volunteers interacted with the students and distributed school materials. The joy and gratitude of the students and teachers were great, highlighting the positive impact of our efforts. This initiative reflects our commitment to social responsibility and educational equity. We plan to continue our support and assistance to help the school further and ensure the students have the resources they need for a better educational experience.

 

 

INAUGURATION OF SCRAP WRAP PROGRAM

On July 10, the NSS unit launched the Scrap Paper Collection campaign known as "Scrap Wrap," and was inaugurated by Principal Fr. Reji P. Kurien. In his address, he emphasised the importance of reducing, reusing, and recycling as key principles of sustainable development. As part of this initiative, scrap paper will be collected from various departments and recycled. This campaign not only marks the beginning of the NSS sustainable development programs but also aims to foster environmental responsibility within the college community. The funds generated from recycling the collected scrap paper will be used to support Snehaveedu, a project dedicated to building homes for homeless people, furthering our commitment to community service and sustainable practices.

 

CLASS BY VOLUNTEERS FOR L.P SCHOOL, THURUTHY

A special class was conducted for the young students of our adopted L.P. School in Thuruthy. Led by our volunteers, the session focused on helping the children overcome stage fear and build confidence in public speaking. During the class, the students learned techniques to manage stage fright through interactive activities. Following this, the children were invited to speak on stage and answer questions, providing them with practical public speaking experience. The program was a success, with the children showing noticeable improvements and expressed their happiness. This initiative effectively fostered confidence and personal growth among the students.

 

 

FOUNDATION STONE LAYING CEREMONY IN KURICHI

As part of our ongoing Snehaveedu project, the NSS unit of St. Berchmans College held a foundation stone laying ceremony for another house in Kurichi on July 26. This marks the seventh house foundation we have initiated under this project, furthering our commitment to providing shelter for those in need. The ceremony was a significant milestone, symbolizing the realization of yet another family's dream of owning a home. By laying this foundation, we have taken a crucial step towards building a safe and secure living space for the family. The foundation stone laying in Kurichi is not just the beginning of a new house; it is the beginning of a brighter future for another deserving family.

 

EXECUTION OF SCRAP WRAP PROGRAM

The NSS unit of St. Berchmans College recently completed a successful 10-day Scrap Wrap Project, focused on promoting environmental sustainability through the collection, sorting, and recycling of scrap paper from various college departments. The project began with an inauguration by Principal Fr. Reji P. Kurian, who emphasized the importance of sustainable practices like reducing, reusing, and recycling.

Throughout the project, our dedicated volunteers collected scrap paper from multiple departments, working diligently to gather and store the materials. The volunteers then carefully sorted the papers into categories such as newspapers, notebooks, and office documents. This organized approach ensured that the recycling process would be efficient and effective.

In addition to fostering environmental awareness, the funds generated from the recycling of the collected scrap paper are earmarked for the Snehaveedu project, which builds homes for homeless families. This initiative not only helped in reducing waste but also contributed to a meaningful cause, reflecting our commitment to both sustainability and social responsibility.

The Scrap Wrap Project successfully engaged the college community, demonstrating how collective efforts can lead to significant positive impacts on both the environment and society. The NSS unit is proud of the accomplishments of this initiative and looks forward to continuing such efforts in the future.
